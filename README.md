## Understanding Sports Betting Types in 1of1DFS

  

To fully appreciate what 1of1DFS Sports offers, it's important to understand the various betting markets they cover:

  

### Straight Bets

These are single wagers on individual games or events:

  

**Moneyline:** Simply picking which team wins outright. For example, if the Lakers are -150 favorites against the Celtics at +130, you'd need to bet $150 on the Lakers to win $100, while a $100 bet on the Celtics would win $130.

  

**Point Spread:** Betting on the margin of victory. If the Chiefs are -7.5 point favorites, they must win by 8 or more points for spread bets to cash. The underdog (+7.5) can lose by 7 or fewer points, or win outright.

  

**Over/Under (Totals):** Wagering on combined scoring. If an NBA game has a total of 225.5 points, you bet whether both teams will score more (over) or fewer (under) total points.

  

### Advanced Betting Strategies

  

**Parlay Bets:** Combining multiple picks for higher payouts, but all selections must win. A 3-team parlay might pay 6-to-1 odds, but if any single bet loses, the entire parlay fails.

  

**Teaser Bets:** Similar to parlays but you can adjust point spreads in your favor for reduced payouts. In a 6-point NFL teaser, you might move a -7 favorite to -1 and a +3 underdog to +9.

  

**Proposition Bets:** Specific player or game statistics. Examples include betting on whether Patrick Mahomes throws for over 2.5 touchdown passes or if LeBron James records a triple-double.

  

**Futures Bets:** Long-term wagers like championship winners or season awards. These typically offer higher payouts early in the season when outcomes are less certain.

  

**Live Betting:** Placing wagers while games are in progress, with odds constantly updating based on game flow. This requires quick decision-making but can offer excellent value for experienced bettors.

  

1of1DFS Sports provides picks across all these betting types, with experts explaining which situations favor each strategy.

### United States Legal Framework

  

Sports betting legality in the United States has changed dramatically in recent years. Previously, the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA) made sports betting illegal in most states.

  

PASPA was struck down by the Supreme Court in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018, allowing individual states to legalize sports betting.

  

### Current State of Legalization

  

As of 2024, sports betting is legal and operational in:

-   37 states plus Washington D.C. and Puerto Rico
-   30 states allow online sports betting
-   Some states only permit betting at physical sportsbooks
